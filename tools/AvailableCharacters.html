Introduction

CTR system applications internally use Unicode (little-endian) so you can display a wide variety of characters, but the types of characters differ among regions.
Below are the lists of characters allowed for such uses as the names of titles and the descriptions of applications.

Some characters may not display correctly depending on your browser environment.
The display results in the various browsers are described below.
(This might vary depending on the environment, so the display results are not guaranteed.)

List of Characters Allowed in Japan, the Americas, Europe, and Australia

The following 7374 characters can be used in title names and publisher names in the Japan, the Americas, the European, and the Australian regions.

In addition to these characters, the single-byte space (Space 0x0020), the double-byte space (Ideographic space 0x3000), and the non-breaking space (No-break space 0x00a0) can also be used.

List of Characters Allowed in Taiwan/Hong Kong

The following 14210 characters can be used in title names and publisher names in the Taiwan/Hong Kong region.

In addition to these characters, the single-byte space (Space 0x0020) and the double-byte space (Ideographic space 0x3000) can also be used.
Note: The non-breaking space (No-break space 0x00a0) cannot be used in the Taiwan/Hong Kong region.
